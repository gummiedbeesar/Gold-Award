The College Research Tool

How To Use This Website

There is no "right" or "wrong" way to use this website. The college research and application process is different for everyone. If you see something that you think will benefit you, have at it! If you see something that you feel won't be helpful, ignore it! I have provided some basic templates that you can use as a starting point, but this tool is all about what works for YOU! With that being said, I will still insert my personal advice and experiences that you can use to make your decisions.

I have provided a list of resources along with what they are helpful for. Some resources will help you research schools, narrow down your list, or even match you with a college advisor.

Senior Year Timeline

Below is a general timeline that you can follow for the college application process. You don't need to follow this exactly to be "successful", so don't be more stressed if you don't want to. This is just what I personally feel is best.

  • Summer:

    • • Make a college email.

    • Learn your Social Security Number!

    • Create a CommonApp account.

    • Create an FSA ID.

    • Make a list of teachers/adults you want to ask for a Letters of Recommendation.

    • Narrow down your college list (if you have one already) to 8-10 schools or less.

    • Think of people you would want to review your essays (preferably adults).

    • Familiarize yourself with what college applications will ask for (College Applications in a Nutshell).

  • Quarter 1 (August to Mid-October):

    • • Start on applications as soon as possible.

    • Ask your list of recommenders. This gives them time to start, or at least puts it into their mind.

    • Get your personal essay done by the end of Q1.

    • Start supplemental essays in August, and start wrapping them up before Fall Break.

    • Think about if/where you want to apply Early Decision. Legally binding and you get a response faster than Regular Decision.

    • Think about if/where you want to apply Early Action. Not legally binding and you get a response faster that Regular Decision.

    • October: COMPLETE FAFSA & CSS AS SOON AS POSSIBLE.

  • Quarter 2 (Mid-October to December):

    • • Complete necessary supplemental essays.

    • Submit any Early Action/Early Decision applications.

    • Submit Regular Decision applications you care the most about (I suggest as many as possible); If you submit them all, you will be done before 2nd semester senior year (when senioritis hits the hardest).

    • Submit FAFSA by NOV 15.

    • Prepare for college interviews (if applicable to you).

    • If you are taking AP tests, study up!
