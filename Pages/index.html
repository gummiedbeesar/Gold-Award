How To Use This Website

There is no "right" or "wrong" way to use this website. The college research and application process is different for everyone. If you see something that you think will benefit you, have at it! And if you see something that you feel won't be helpful, ignore it! I have provided some basic templates that you can use as a starting point, but this tool is all about what works for YOU!

With that being said, I do have suggestions based on my experience :D

I have provided a list of resources along with what they are helpful for. Some resources will help you research schools, narrow down your list, or even match you with a college advisor.

Senior Year Timeline

Below is a general timeline that you can follow for this process. In my opinion, it is a perferable timeline, but I can't tell you what to do. You also don't need to follow it exactly to be "successful", so don't be more stressed if you don't.

  • Summer

    • - Make a college email

    - Learn your Social Security Number!

    - Create a CommonApp account.

    - Create an FSA ID.

    - Make a list of teachers/adults you want to ask for a Letters of Recommendation

    - Narrow down your college list (if you have one already)

    - Think of people you would want to review your essays (preferably adults)

    - Familiarize yourself with what college applications will ask for (College Applications in a Nutshell)

  • Quarter 1 (August - Mid-October)

    • - Start on applications as soon as possible

    - Ask your list of recommenders. This gives them time to start, or at least puts it into their mind

    - Get your personal essay done by the end of Q1

    - If you don‚Äôt have a school that you want to Early Decision to, consider Early Action when available

    - October: COMPLETE FAFSA & CSS AS SOON AS POSSIBLE

  • Quarter 2 (Mid-October - December)

    • - Submit any Early Action/Early Decision applications

    - Submit regular decision applications you care the most about (I really suggest as many as possible); If you submit them all, you will be done before 2nd semester senior year (where senioritis hits the hardest)

    - Submit FAFSA by NOV 15

    - College interviews

    • - Update statuses of schools (on however you are organizing your list)

    - Review financial aid awards

    - Exhale. Breathe.

    - Have mid-year transcript sent in to schools that ask

    - Dive deeper into schools and start deciding which you like the most

    - Keep up with deadlines

  • Quarter 4 (Mid-March - May)

    • - Commit to a school by May 1st. (Typically the deadline schools give)

    - Attend admitted students webinars and things like that!

    - Look at housing options

    - Connect with future students when you can

    - If you are taking AP tests, study up!
