Helpful Resources

Common College Terms

I created this document of common terms used throughout the college research and application process. I highly recommend looking through this because by just knowing what certain terms mean, the entire process is less stressful overall.

College Research Advice (PPT)

This is a powerpoint I made that goes through "How To Research a College" based on what I learned through my experience, meeting with my school counselor, talking with my Matriculate Advising Fellow, and students who have applied and currently go to college.

College General Timeline

Based on my experience and the PowerPoints that I presented, here is a general timeline that you could follow during your application process. Because of this timeline, I was able to be done with a lot of my college stuff before Quarter 3.

College Research Worksheet (ACT)

This is a worksheet that the ACT has for students to use to research a college. I feel that this is a great starting point for when you are initially researching colleges. After you have put together a list of schools that meet your overall criteria, you can begin to narrow it down by seeing what schools offer/have the specific things you are looking for in a school.

College Application Advice (PPT)

This is a powerpoint I made that goes through what your senior year will look like regarding college applications based on what I learned through my experience, meeting with my school counselor, talking with my Matriculate Advising Fellow, and asking students who have applied and currently go to college.

Appily (Cappex)

Appily is a very useful resource for finding colleges that fit a criteria that you set. From there you can use the colleges shown to build your list and research them further.

BigFuture College Search

BigFuture has a really useful CollegeSearch tool! CollegeBoard also shows you what AP credits transfer to what college/university. They also have a college quiz.

College Rover

College Rover is most helpful for comparing colleges side-by-side. The most helpful parts of this website has been pay-walled. There are ways to get around it, but I cannot give that information here.

Niche

Niche was the most helpful resource for me. It is very helpful to use when looking for how schools rank in certain categories (whether it be by a specific major or by "Best Food").

CollegeVine

CollegeVine is another website that can help you find schools in bulk that you can screen through to see if you want to add them to your list or not.

National Center for Education Statistics

NCES is more data heavy and not as user friendly as the other resources listed. It is helpful, though, because you can see statistics about schools from the government.

Common Data Set

There is no link for the Common Data Set because you can find it for each individual school by searching "[school name] common data set". The CDS can show you demographics and what they are looking for in applications.

Matriculate

Connects students with undergraduate advisors to help with college research and application process. I used this program and it was really helpful. I highly recommend it.

QuestBridge

QuestBridge is a program that allows students to match with highly selective schools and receive near-full-ride scholarships to the schools. If you are low income and interested in those top schools, I definitely think you should look into QuestBridge.
