About Me

Hello! My name is Kennedi and I am the creator of this project! Since the 8th grade, I have been obsessed with college move-in vlogs. I would watch hours of content thinking, “Wow that's such a long time from now” and “That's so cool!”. Now, though, I have experienced the true stress of college season and find it is not that cool and has come a little (much) too quickly.

These are my origins, but what truly inspired me to create this was seeing the mental toll it was taking on my peers. In my junior year, I wanted to create a resource that would help people research colleges. I made a template on Notion and thought it was a done deal. Luckily (😐), junior year was very busy so I decided to postpone my project to senior year. After living through the entire process, I felt that this tool needed to be broader. I realized that a lot of the college process is unknown, and that is what makes it so scary. No matter when you start, you will feel behind or discouraged. Deadlines feel like they are far away until they are around the corner. The personal essay feels impossible to write (I mean, seriously, who came up with that.) Most of all, everyone is asking, “So what school do you want to go to?” and you have to awkwardly answer, “Oh haha, I don't know yet” and then they either say, “Oh it's okay! You have plenty of time” or “It's almost time to apply!” then you have to laugh it off and hope they don't bring it up again because 11th grade felt like just yesterday and you didn't fully realize how fast this whole thing would creep up on you. Hahaha! Right? What was I saying?

I created this website to provide starting points for students interested in pursuing a 4-year postsecondary institution. There are some tips and information from myself, counselors, undergraduate advisors, friends, recent high school graduates, and YouTube. There are links to a lot of helpful resources with explanations of how to use them. Lastly, there are some templates I created that could help throughout your application process.

I hope this helps, even if it's only a little. I believe in you, and good luck!

- Kennedi W. '24
