  • expand_moreCollege Apps in a Nutshell
    file_downloadDownload Word Document Google Doc Version

    The cool part about college applications is that apart from essays, they are all the same. They ask for your personal information, family, education, and awards. If you use things like CommonApp or Scoir, the most common streamlined college applications, you will come across these questions. Furthermore, it will get either annoying or rewarding depending on where you are in the process. For many people, if you are in the stage of your application where you are only filling in this information, it gets annoying very quickly. But, when you get to the part of yout journey where you are only writing 500-600 word essays, this part magically becomes less annoying.

    I created this template after filling out many college and scholarship applications. Most of them asked questions like these which while they were not difficult to answer, they weren't easily remembered. For instance, I know my name by heart (yay!) but I don't know the exact date I started my summer job. Another use for this is keeping your information consistant, like club hours, rather than trying to calculate each time. Most times when you are asked about club participation it is asked in hours per week and weeks per year. Some clubs meet for different times depending on the year, so it's harder to provide an average to stick with. THE MOST IMPORTANT THING TO REMEMBER IS THAT YOU SHOULD NOT BLATANTLY LIE BY OVEREXAGGERATING.
